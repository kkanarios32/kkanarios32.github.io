Review: Genius Makers [OGCRX]

  • February 8, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

This was basically a historical account of the history of deep learning. The main focus was championing the likes of Hinton, Lecun, and Bengio with their unwavering conviction toward deep learning. Honestly, this book has made me excited and a bit intimidated about research. I do not know if I have the same obsessive commitment to my work as Hinton, etc. Also, for future reference, I feel like I will get a lot more out of books I am not already so familiar with. Technically, I thought it did an OK job, but I once again do not really enjoy reading books on my field for a broad audience. Learning about the industry origins i.e. DNN, Deepmind acquisition, etc. was pretty interesting though.

Enjoyable read. 5/10.

        Review: The Mezzanine [HGRL]

        • July 15, 2026
        • Kellen Kanarios

        This was a very different read for me. A short book (only 135 pages) that goes into great detail the work day at the Mezzanine. I have some gripes with the description of readable on the cover. I found the incredibly vivid imagery sometimes hard to follow. It seems the intent of the book was to highlight the thoughts of an ordinary working man throughout the day. However, some of these thoughs were so far from ordinary it lost the relatable, funny read it was going for. Some chapters were pure gold (the urinal) but others were a huge miss for me.

        Overall, I enjoyed the book and would recommend. 6.5/10.

        Review: The Infinity Machine [OGCRW]

        • May 20, 2026
        • Kellen Kanarios

        Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed this book. As a reinforcement learning afficianado, I have a newfound fandom for the mission of Deepmind. In particular, I was at the RLC talk given by David Silver mentioned in the book. However, I must acknowledge the deep bias in favor of Demis and Deepmind in the LLM race towards the end. It is easy to come away from this, thinking that Sam Altman is some sort of Silicon Valley super villain and Demis is our champion of humanity.

        If I had to give an unbiased rating, I would give this book a 6.5/10. However, as a biased reader, I give it an 8/10 :)
