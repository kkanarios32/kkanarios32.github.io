Binary classification [3Q40]

  • December 28, 2025
  • A classifier is a function mapping from \(\mathcal {X}\) to \(\{-1, 1\} \).
  • The nearest neighbor classifier maps some \(\mathbf {x}\) to the label of its nearest neighbor.
  • The \(k\)-nearest neighbor classifier takes the majority of the \(k\)-nearest neighbors
    1. What is the effect of \(k\)?
    2. \(k\) affects smoothness and therefore the tradeoff between overfitting and underfitting.

To introduce the algorithms that follow, we must first introduce the bayes-optimal classifier. To do so, they formulate this as a problem of representing the joint distribution of \((X,Y)\), where \(Y\) is the class label and \(X\) is the observation. They propose two decompositions:

  1. Class posterior \(P_{Y|x}\) and data marginal \(P_X\)
  2. Class conditional \(P_{X|y}\) and class prior \(P_Y\)
