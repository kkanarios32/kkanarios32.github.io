Theorem. Second-order approximation [003O]

  • January 27, 2025

Let \(f:\mathbb {R}^{n}\to \mathbb {R}\) be twice continuously differentiable, and suppose \(\nabla ^{2}f(\mathbf {x})\) is \(\gamma _{Q}(\mathbf {x}_{0})\)-Lipschitz continuous at \(\mathbf {x}_{0}\), then \[\left |f(\mathbf {x})-\widehat {f}_{Q}(\mathbf {x};\mathbf {x}_{0})\right |\ \leq \ \frac {\gamma _{Q}(\mathbf {x}_{0})}{6}\left ||\mathbf {x}-\mathbf {x}_{0}\right ||_{2}^{3},\] where we define \[\widehat {f}_{Q}(\mathbf {x};\mathbf {x}_{0}):=f(\mathbf {x}_{0})+\langle \nabla f(\mathbf {x}_{0}),\mathbf {x}-\mathbf {x}_{0}\rangle \] \[+\frac {1}{2}(\mathbf {x}-\mathbf {x}_{0})^{\top }\nabla ^{2}f(\mathbf {x}_{0})(\mathbf {x}-\mathbf {x}_{0}).\]

    Definition. Lipschitz Continous [003J]

    • January 27, 2025

    Suppose that

    • \(f:\mathcal {X}\mapsto \mathcal {Y}\) with \(\mathcal {X}\) and \(\mathcal {Y}\) being open sets;
    • \(||\cdot ||_{\mathcal {X}}\) and \(||\cdot ||_{\mathcal {Y}}\) are norms on \(\mathcal {X}\) and \(\mathcal {Y}\), respectively.
    \(f(\cdot )\) is Lipschitz continuous over \(\mathcal {X}\) if \(\exists L<\infty \,,\) such that \[||f(\boldsymbol {y})-f(\boldsymbol {x})||_{\mathcal {Y}}\ \leq \ L(\boldsymbol {x})\,||\boldsymbol {y}-\boldsymbol {x}||_{\mathcal {X}}\,,\quad \forall \boldsymbol {x},\ \boldsymbol {y}\in \mathcal {X}.\]

    Theorem. Mean Value Theorem I [003L]

    • January 27, 2025

    Let \(f:\mathbb {R}^{n}\mapsto \mathbb {R}\) be continuously differentiable. For any fixed \(\mathbf {x}\) and \(\mathbf {y}\), we have \[\,f(\mathbf {y})\,=\,f(\mathbf {x})\,+\,\langle \nabla f(\mathbf {z}(t_{L})),\mathbf {y}-\mathbf {x}\rangle \,\] for some \(\mathbf {z}(t_{L})=(1-t_{L})\cdot \mathbf {x}+t_{L}\cdot \mathbf {y}\) with \(t_{L}\in (0,1)\,\).

    Remark.
    • If \(f:\mathbb {R}^n\mapsto \mathbb {R}\) is continuously differentiable, \[\begin {aligned} |f(\pmb {x})-f(\pmb {y})| &\leq & \sup _{\pmb {z} \in \mathbb {R}^n} ||\nabla f(\pmb {z})||_2 \, ||\pmb {x}-\pmb {y}||_2 \end {aligned}\] so that the Lipschitz constant of \(f\) is \(\sup _{\pmb {z} \in \mathbb {R}^n} ||\nabla f(\pmb {z})||_2\).
    • If \(f:\mathbb {R}^{n}\mapsto \mathbb {R}\) is twice continuously differentiable, \[||\nabla f(\boldsymbol {x})-\nabla f(\boldsymbol {y})||_{2}\ \leq \ \sup _{\boldsymbol {z}\in \mathbb {R}^{n}}||\nabla ^{2}f(\boldsymbol {z})||_{2}\,||\boldsymbol {x}-\boldsymbol {y}||_{2}\] so that the Lipschitz constant of \(\nabla f\) is \(\sup _{\boldsymbol {z}\in \mathbb {R}^{n}}||\nabla ^{2}f(\boldsymbol {z})||_{2}\)

    Theorem. Mean Value Theorem II [003M]

    • January 27, 2025

    Let \(f:\mathbb {R}^{n}\mapsto \mathbb {R}\) be twice continuously differentiable. For any fixed \(\mathbf {x}\) and \(\mathbf {y}\), we have \[\nabla f(\mathbf {y})\;=\;\nabla f(\mathbf {x})\,+\,\int _{0}^{1}\nabla ^{2}f(\mathbf {z}(t))\cdot (\mathbf {y}-\mathbf {x})dt\] for some \(\mathbf {z}(t)=(1-t)\mathbf {x}+t\mathbf {y}\) with \(t\in (0,1)\,,\) and that \[\boxed {f(\mathbf {y})\;=\;f(\mathbf {x})+(\nabla f(\mathbf {x}),\mathbf {y}-\mathbf {x})+\frac {1}{2}(\mathbf {y}-\mathbf {x})^{\top }\nabla ^{2}f(\mathbf {z}(t_{Q}))(\mathbf {y}-\mathbf {x})}\] for some \(t_{Q}\in (0,1)\,.\)

      Newton's Method [004V]

      • February 3, 2025

      Consider second-order taylor approximation of \(f\) \[ f(\mathbf {x}_{k}+\mathbf {d})\ \approx \ \underbrace {f(\mathbf {x}_{k})+\mathbf {d}^{\top }\nabla f(\mathbf {x}_{k})+{\frac {1}{2}}\mathbf {d}^{\top }\nabla ^{2}f(\mathbf {x}_{k})\mathbf {d}}_{Q_k( \mathbf {d})}\] Then setting \(\nabla Q_k( \mathbf {d})\) and solving for \(\mathbf {d}\), we get \[ \nabla f( \mathbf {x}_k) + \frac {1}{2}\nabla ^2 f( \mathbf {x}_k) \mathbf {d} = 0 \] Thus, take Newton's direction \[\mathbf {d}_{k}^{N}=-\left (\nabla ^{2}f(\mathbf {x}_{k})\right )^{-1}\nabla f(\mathbf {x}_{k}).\]

      Remark. Some notes
      • Fast convergence: Newton's method usually has local quadratic convergence.
      • Expensive: requires storing and inverting \(\nabla ^2 f( \mathbf {x})\).
      • Unstable: \(\nabla ^2 f \succ 0\) might not hold for general nonlinear problems.
