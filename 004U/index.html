Theorem. Linear convergence for strong convexity [004U]

  • February 3, 2025
  • Kellen Kanarios

Let \(f:\mathbb {R}^{n}\mapsto \mathbb {R}\) be smooth and \(\mu \)-strongly convex, and suppose its gradient \(\nabla f(\mathbf {x})\) is \(L\)-Lipschitz. Choose a fixed step size \(\tau _{k}\equiv \tau =\frac {2}{\mu +L}\), then \[\left ||\mathbf {x}_{k}-\mathbf {x}_{\star }\right ||_{2}\ \leq \ \left (\frac {\kappa -1}{\kappa +1}\right )^{k}\left ||\mathbf {x}_{0}-\mathbf {x}_{\star }\right ||_{2},\] where \(\kappa =\frac {L}{\mu }\) is the condition number, and \(\mathbf {x}_{\star }\) is the minimizer.
