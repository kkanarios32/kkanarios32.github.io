Definition. \(R\)-convergence [003S]

  • January 27, 2025

We say the \(R\)-convergence is of order \(p\;(\geq 1)\), \[\min _{\mathbf {x}_{*}\in \mathcal {X}}||\mathbf {x}_{k}-\mathbf {x}_{*}||_{2}\;\leq \;\rho _{k},\] if the sequence \(\{\rho _{k}\}_{k\geq 1}\) is \(Q\)-convergence of the order \(p\).

Context

    Rate of Convergence [DER5]

    • July 6, 2025

    Want to find \[\operatorname *{min}_{\mathbf {x}}\ f(\mathbf {x}),\quad \text {s.t.}\quad \mathbf {x}\ \in \ \mathcal {C}.\] Solve the problem via iterative methods of optimization, which produce a sequence of points \[\mathbf {x}_{1},\ \mathbf {x}_{2},\ \cdots ,\mathbf {x}_{k},\cdots \] starting from an initialization \(\mathbf {x}_{0}\).

    Definition. First-order convergence [003R]

    • January 27, 2025

    First-order function value convergence \[||\nabla f(\mathbf {x}_k)||_2 \; \leq \; \varepsilon .\]

    • For convex functions, this also means convergence to the global minimizer
    • For nonconvex functions, this only means convergence to a Stationary point (e.g., a local minimizer or a saddle point).

    Definition. \(Q\)-convergence [003Q]

    • January 27, 2025

    We say the \(Q\)-convergence is of order \((p \geq 1)\) and with factor \((\gamma > 0)\), if \(\exists k_{0}\), such that \(\forall k\geq k_{0}\): \[\boxed {\min _{\mathbf {x_{*}}\in \mathcal {X}}||\mathbf {x_{k}}-\mathbf {x_{*}}||_{2}\ \leq \ \gamma \cdot \left (\min _{\mathbf {x_{*}}\in \mathcal {X}}||\mathbf {x_{k-1}}-\mathbf {x_{*}}||_{2}\right )^{p}.}\]
