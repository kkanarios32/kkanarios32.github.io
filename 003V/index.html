Theorem. Chain Rule of Mutual Info [003V]

  • January 29, 2025
  • Kellen Kanarios

We have that \[ \begin {align*} I(X, Y; Z) = I(X; Z) + I(Y; Z | X) \end {align*} \]

Proof. By definition, \[ \begin {align*} I(X, Y; Z) &= H(X,Y) + H(Z) - H(X, Y, Z) \\ &= H(X) + H(Y | X) + H(Z) - H(X, Z) - H(Y | X, Z) \\ &= H(X) + H(Y | X) + H(Z) - H(X, Z) - H(Y | X, Z) \\ &= I(X; Z) + H(Y | X) - (H(Y, Z | X) - H(Z | X)) \\ &= I(X; Z) + I(Y; Z | X) \end {align*} \] as desired
