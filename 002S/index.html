Definition. Linear MDP [002S]

  • January 15, 2025
  • Kellen Kanarios

An MDP \(\mathcal {M}=(\mathcal {S},\mathcal {A},H,P,r)\) is called a linear MDP if there exists two feature maps \(\phi :\mathcal {S}\times \mathcal {A}\rightarrow \mathbb {R}^{d}\), \(\mu :\mathcal {S}\rightarrow \mathbb {R}^{d}\) and a vector \(\theta _{r}\in \mathbb {R}^{d}\), such that \[P(s^{\prime }|s,a)=\langle \phi (s,a),\mu (s^{\prime })\rangle ,\quad r(s,a)=\langle \phi (s,a),\theta _{r}\rangle \] and \(\phi \) and \(\mu \) also satisfy the normalization conditions: \[ \begin {align*} &\forall (s,a),\ \lVert \phi (s,a) \rVert _{2} \leq 1,\ \lVert \theta _{r}\rVert _{2} \leq \sqrt {d} \\ &\forall g:\mathcal {S}\rightarrow \mathbb {R},\ \lVert g \rVert _{L_{\infty }}\leq 1\ \Rightarrow \left \lVert \int _{\mathcal {S}}\mu (s^{\prime })g(s^{\prime })ds^{\prime }\right \rVert _{2}\leq \sqrt {d} \end {align*} \]
