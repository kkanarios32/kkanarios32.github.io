Theorem. Characterizations of sub-Gaussian random variables [002M]

  • January 15, 2025
  • Kellen Kanarios

Let \(X\) be a random variable. Then the following are equivalent.

  1. \(X\) is sub-Gaussian;
  2. \(\exists a > 0\) such that \(\mathbb {E}(\exp (aX^2)) < \infty \);
  3. \(\exists \ell \), \(\forall \lambda > 0\), \(\mathbb {E}(\exp (\lambda X)) \leq X \exp (\lambda ^2 \ell )\);
  4. \(\exists K' > 0\) \(\forall p > 1\) \((\mathbb {E}|X|^{p})^{1/p} \leq K' \sqrt {p}\).
