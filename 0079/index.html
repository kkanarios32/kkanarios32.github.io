    Definition. Riemannian Manifold [0072]

    • April 14, 2025

    A Riemannian manifold \( (\mathcal {M},\langle \cdot ,\cdot \rangle ) \) is a smooth manifold \( \mathcal {M} \) equipped with positive-definite inner product \( \langle \cdot ,\cdot \rangle \) on the tangent space \( \mathrm {T}_{\pmb {x}}\mathcal {M} \) at each point \( \pmb {x}\in \mathcal {M} \).

    • April 14, 2025

    Definition. Riemannian Gradient [0077]

    • April 14, 2025

    Let \( f:\mathcal {M}\mapsto \mathbb {R} \) be smooth on the Riemannian manifold \( \mathcal {M} \). The Riemannian gradient \( \,\operatorname {grad}f(\boldsymbol {x})\, \) of \( f \) at \( \boldsymbol {x}\in \mathcal {M} \) is the unique tangent vector at \( \boldsymbol {x} \) such that \[ \mathrm {D}f(\boldsymbol {x})[\boldsymbol {v}]\ =\ \langle \operatorname {grad}f(\boldsymbol {x}),\boldsymbol {v}\rangle _{\boldsymbol {x}}\,,\quad \forall \,\boldsymbol {v}\in \mathrm {T}_{\boldsymbol {x}}\mathcal {M}. \] By extending the definition of \( f \) to the ambient Euclidean space \( \mathbb {R}^{n} \), we can show that \[\mathrm {grad} f(\mathbf {x}) = \mathcal {P}_{T_x \mathcal {M}}(\nabla f(\mathbf {x}))\] where \( {\mathcal {P}}_{\mathrm {T}_{\mathbf {x}}\mathcal {M}}\) is the orthogonal projector from \(\mathbb {R}^n\) to \(T_x \mathcal {M}\)
