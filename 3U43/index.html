Definition. Spectral norm [3U43]

  • July 11, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

The operator norm of a matrix induced by the Euclidean norm on both sides, \(\|\mathbf {A}\|_{2\to 2}\), usually written just \(\|\mathbf {A}\|\). It equals the largest singular value, \[\|\mathbf {A}\|\ =\ \sup _{\|\mathbf {x}\|_2\leq 1}\|\mathbf {A}\mathbf {x}\|_2\ =\ \sigma _1(\mathbf {A}),\] i.e. the most any unit vector can be stretched by \(\mathbf {A}\). As a Schatten norm it is the \(p=\infty \) case, \(\|\mathbf {A}\|_{\mathrm {S}_\infty }=\|\sigma (\mathbf {A})\|_\infty \), sitting opposite the Frobenius norm (\(p=2\)) in that family.

    Definition. Unitary Invariant Matrix Norm [003H]

    • January 27, 2025
    • Kellen Kanarios

    Let \(\mathbf {A}\in \mathbb {R}^{m\times n}.\) We say the matrix norm is unitary invariant if \[\left ||\mathbf {A}\right ||_{\sharp }\ =\ \left ||\mathbf {PAQ}\right ||_{\sharp },\quad \forall \ \mathbf {P}\in O(m),\ \mathbf {Q}\in O(n).\]

    The two most common examples are both unitary invariant because they depend on \(\mathbf {A}\) only through its singular values: the Spectral norm (\(\|\sigma (\mathbf {A})\|_\infty \)) and the Frobenius norm (\(\|\sigma (\mathbf {A})\|_2\)).

    Definition. Frobenius norm [4M9J]

    • July 11, 2026
    • Kellen Kanarios

    The entrywise Euclidean norm of a matrix — treat \(\mathbf {A}\) as a long vector and take its 2-norm: \[\|\mathbf {A}\|_F\ =\ \sqrt {\sum _{i,j}A_{ij}^2}\ =\ \sqrt {\langle \mathbf {A},\mathbf {A}\rangle }\ =\ \sqrt {\mathrm {tr}(\mathbf {A}^{\top }\mathbf {A})}.\] It is the norm induced by the matrix inner product, and in terms of singular values it is the \(p=2\) Schatten norm, \(\|\mathbf {A}\|_F=\sqrt {\sum _i\sigma _i^2(\mathbf {A})}\) — the Euclidean length of the singular-value vector, dual in that family to the spectral norm (\(p=\infty \)).

        Definition. Schatten \(p\)-norm [003I]

        • January 27, 2025
        • Kellen Kanarios

        Let \(\mathbf {A}\in \mathbb {R}^{m\times n}\). For any \(p \in [1,+\infty ]\), the function \[\left ||\mathbf {A}|\right |_{\mathrm {S}_{p}}\,:=\,\left ||\mathbf {\sigma }(\mathbf {A})|\right |_{p}\] is a norm on \(\,\mathbb {R}^{m\times n}\).
