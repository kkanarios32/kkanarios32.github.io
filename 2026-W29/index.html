1. The Quantified Me: 2026-W29 [NQFF]

  • July 20, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

This week I spent the large majority of my time taking notes in this forest. We can see this via the 23hr spent on my forest throughout the week. Admittedly, some of this was spent purely on aesthetic re-design / cleanup, which I hope to not have to do this week. Lab corresponds to my kernel learning, where I spent what appears to be ~5hr implementing a few of the Triton tutorials, including GEMM. I played around with excalidraw and took some notes here. With this, I began looking into the Kernel DSLs landscape along with IR7B.

Outside of coding, I did not spend too much time brain-rotting. My phone screentime has also gotten remarkably low, so the issue is not really wasting time doomscrolling / watching youtube, etc. Instead, the problem is just a lack of maintained focus. I bounce between things too frequently, failing to get as much done as I should. I hope to remedy this by being disciplined with pomodoro blocks but early attempts have not been too promising.

2. Life Maxing: 2026-W29 [QBM9]

  • July 20, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

I have been on a remarkable streak of bad luck when trying to run. During vacation (the 3 mile week), there was a heatwave going on throughout Europe. Then, upon my return, I got wildly sick (still managed to get to 30 miles but my Garmin did not like it). Then, this most recent week the Canadian wildfires made Michigan look like a scene out of Chernobyl. I tried to run, but my eyes began watering as soon as I got outside. Hopefully, I can get back on track and scale back up to 40mpw and then to 50+ before we get too close to the marathon.

In other news, I seem to be getting pretty consistent with my habits. I have remarkably caught up on my Anki deck and now need to do more exploratory study to have enough cards each day. The to-do list has not been as life-changing as I hoped as I still struggle to adhere to it on a daily basis. One reason is I think I am too ambitious and should instead start with just one must get done thing each day.

3. Reading Corner: 2026-W29 [K7MF]

  • July 20, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

I have begun reading Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Goden Braid and am really enjoying it. It is strangely refreshing having the math I learned so rigorously distilled into much more digestible chunks. I have also gotten halfway through Candide, which is a very different read (thoughts coming soon).

Review: The Mezzanine [HGRL]

  • July 15, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

This was a very different read for me. A short book (only 135 pages) that goes into great detail the work day at the Mezzanine. I have some gripes with the description of readable on the cover. I found the incredibly vivid imagery sometimes hard to follow. It seems the intent of the book was to highlight the thoughts of an ordinary working man throughout the day. However, some of these thoughs were so far from ordinary it lost the relatable, funny read it was going for. Some chapters were pure gold (the urinal) but others were a huge miss for me.

Overall, I enjoyed the book and would recommend. 6.5/10.

4. Goals for Next Week

  • July 17, 2026
  • Kellen Kanarios

I completed a rough outline of the state of kernel generations as far as I am concerned. I hope to make some serious progress on this next week. Namely, I want to have a pretty complete understanding of the Tiled DSLs portion and where the distinction between each DSL is made. I would like to deeply understand the Flash attention Triton kernel from the tutorial. Afterwards, moving to TileLang and seeing how / where they draw the line for more optimization opportunity.

For Reading Corner, I will likely finish Candide and might also finish Elon Musk, which I have been working on for awhile now. Stay tuned for their reviews!

                Review: The Infinity Machine [OGCRW]

                • May 20, 2026
                • Kellen Kanarios

                Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed this book. As a reinforcement learning afficianado, I have a newfound fandom for the mission of Deepmind. In particular, I was at the RLC talk given by David Silver mentioned in the book. However, I must acknowledge the deep bias in favor of Demis and Deepmind in the LLM race towards the end. It is easy to come away from this, thinking that Sam Altman is some sort of Silicon Valley super villain and Demis is our champion of humanity.

                If I had to give an unbiased rating, I would give this book a 6.5/10. However, as a biased reader, I give it an 8/10 :)

                Review: Genius Makers [OGCRX]

                • February 8, 2026
                • Kellen Kanarios

                This was basically a historical account of the history of deep learning. The main focus was championing the likes of Hinton, Lecun, and Bengio with their unwavering conviction toward deep learning. Honestly, this book has made me excited and a bit intimidated about research. I do not know if I have the same obsessive commitment to my work as Hinton, etc. Also, for future reference, I feel like I will get a lot more out of books I am not already so familiar with. Technically, I thought it did an OK job, but I once again do not really enjoy reading books on my field for a broad audience. Learning about the industry origins i.e. DNN, Deepmind acquisition, etc. was pretty interesting though.

                Enjoyable read. 5/10.
