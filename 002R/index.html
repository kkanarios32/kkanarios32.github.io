Holder's Inequality [002R]

  • January 15, 2025
  • Kellen Kanarios

Let \((S,\Sigma ,\mu )\) be a measure space and let \(p,q\in [1,\ \infty ]\) with \(1/p+1/q=1\). Then for all measurable real- or complex-valued functions \(f\) and \(g\) on \(S\), \[\lVert fg \rVert \leq \lVert f \rVert _{p}\lVert g \rVert _{q}.\] If, in addition, \(p,q\in (1,\ \infty )\) and \(f\in L^{q}(\mu )\) and \(g\in L^{q}(\mu )\), then Holder's inequality becomes an equality if and only if \(\lVert f\rVert ^{p}\) and \(\lVert g\rVert ^{q}\) are linearly dependent in \(L^{1}(\mu )\), meaning that there exist real numbers \(a,\,\beta \geq 0\), not both of them zero, such that \(\lVert f\rVert ^{p}=\beta \,\lVert g\rVert ^{q}\) \(\mu \)-almost everywhere.
