Definition. Sub-Gaussian Random Variables [002L]

  • January 15, 2025

A random variable \(X\) is called \(K\)-Sub-Gaussian if \(\forall t > 0\) \[\mathbb {P}(|X| > t) \leq C\exp \left (-c\left (\frac {t}{k}^2\right )\right )\]

    Theorem. Hoeffding's Inequality [002N]

    • January 15, 2025

    Let \(X_1, \ldots , X_n\) be independent centered Sub-Gaussian Random Variables. Then for any \(a = (a_1, \ldots , a_n) \in \mathbb {R}^n\) and for any \(t > 0\) \[\mathbb {P}\left (\left |\sum _{k = 1}^{n} a_j X_j\right | > t \right ) \leq 2 \exp \left (-c \frac {t^2}{\lVert a\rVert ^2}\right )\]

    Definition. Sub-Gaussian Norm [002P]

    • January 15, 2025

    For \(p > 0\), define \[\lVert X \rVert _{\psi _p} = \inf \left \{K > 0 \mid \mathbb {E} \exp ^{\left (\frac {X}{k}\right )^p}\right \}\] If \(X\) is Sub-Gaussian Random Variables then this is well defined and makes our space a Banach Space.

    Corollary. khinchin's Inequality [002O]

    • January 15, 2025

    Let \(X_1, \ldots , X_n\) be i.i.d. centered Sub-Gaussian Random Variables such that \(\mathbb {E} X^2 = 1\). Then for any \(p \in [1, \infty ]\), there exists \(A_p, B_p > 0\) such that \(\forall a = (a_1, \ldots , a_n) \in \mathbb {R}^n\) \[A_p \lVert a \rVert _2 \leq \left (\mathbb {E}\left |\sum _{j = 1}^{n} a_j X_j\right |^p\right )^{1/p} \leq B_p \lVert a \rVert _2\]
