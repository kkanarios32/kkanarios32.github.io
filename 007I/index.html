Definition. Multivariate Normal Distribution [007I]

  • April 22, 2025
  • Kellen Kanarios

The multivariate normal distribution is defined by the density function \[f(\mathbf {x}) = (2\pi )^{-k/2}\operatorname *{det}(\mathbf {\Sigma })^{-1/2}\ \exp \left (-\frac {1}{2}(\mathbf {x}-{\boldsymbol {\mu }})^{\mathrm {{T}}}{\boldsymbol {\Sigma }}^{-1}\big (\mathbf {x}-{\boldsymbol {\mu }}\big )\right )\] where \( \mathbf {\Sigma } \) is the covariance matrix \[\mathbf {\Sigma }_{i,j} = \mathbb {E}[(X_i - \mu _i)(X_j - \mu _j)^{\top }]\]and \( \boldsymbol {\mu } \) is the mean vector \[\mathbf {\mu } = (\mathbb {E}[X_1], \ldots , \mathbb {E}[X_n])\].
